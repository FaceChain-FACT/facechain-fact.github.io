Facechain-FACT：face adapter for human aigc

Haoyu Xie1, Yang Liu2, Lei Shang2, Cheng Yu2, Chao Xu2, Fei Wang1,2, Baigui Sun2
1Alibaba Group,
Paper arXiv Video Code Data

Portrait photo generated by FaceChain-FACT portraits.

Abstract

Nowadays, image generation technology has produced many shocking photos. Some subject-driven techniques have also tried to incorporate customized objects into customized content. Due to the high detail and recognition of human faces, face customization poses a greater challenge to image generation.

FaceChain, an outstanding work in task-specific portrait generation, has received widespread attention in the community. It trains a person's LoRA model to incorporate face information, and generates customized portraits. However, due to the need to train users' LoRA models, FaceChain's pipeline is divided into two stages: training and inference, which greatly increases the user's cost. We propose a single-stage version called FaceChain-FACT that does not require LoRA model training. We: 1. Integrate a transformer face feature extractor with the same structure as Stable Diffusion, which better enables SD to utilize face information; 2. Generate using dense fine-grained features, with better replication effects; 3. Seamlessly compatible with ControlNet and LoRA plugins, plug and play.

Method

We use a series of image preprocessing, including face segmentation, face cropping and alignment, hand detection, face quality screening, etc., to screen and obtain a training dataset. We leverage a transformer-based face feature extractor to extract features and utilize the dense fine-grained features from the penultimate layer as the face condition. Stable Diffusion receives the face condition through the FACT-Adapter and combines it with text to generate portrait images. By fusing various LoRA models from FaceChain, it can generate portraits with a variety of styles.
